Pronoma Banerjee

I am a 5th year undergraduate pursuing a double major in Integrated MSc. Mathematics and B.E. Computer Science at BITS Pilani K.K. Birla Goa Campus. I am currently doing my final year thesis at the Oden Institute of Computational Engineering and Sciences, University of Texas at Austin, under Prof. Chandrajit Bajaj

.

My research is focused on Computer Graphics and Reinforcement Learning for shape optimization applied to objects in images and videos, as well as in molecules, to study drug-target interactions as well as animations and improved object detection. I've worked on developing generative frameworks for super-resolution and detection in Hyperspectral and RGB images and videos, and also for correcting noisy models for improved performance. I'm currently exploring RL methods for shape recovery from videos, as well as the problem of finding the accurate minimum energy configuration of 2 molecules interacting with each other, by forming suitable hydrogen bonds, via a double loop Hamiltonian learning framework.

My goal is to advance the state-of-the-art in Computer Vision and Graphics for healthcare, creative arts and animation and I open to any kind of opportunity to intern or collaborate in any project that aims to do this.

Projects

SynthBreeder

Status: Active

Implementing the genetic algorithm on various setups of the modular synthesizer, called "organisms". Each organism produces a particular kind of sound. The organisms evolve by undergoing the graph-based processes of mutation and crossover, and natural selection, resulting in changes in connections and setups, evolving from fragments of sound to a section of a musical piece.

ABC-GAN

Status: Active

Working on a generative modeling paradigm called skipGAN which combines GANs and Approximate Bayesian Computing (ABC-GAN), with skip connections. The architecture aims at correcting likelihood misspecification in prior models. Currently trying to implement our model on TabNet, CatBoost and Stats Model priors, using several synthetic and real-life datasets.

Adversarial Deep Learning

Status: Archived

Contributed actively to the implementations of the various computer vision models, adversarial attacks and defenses on MNIST-10 and CIFAR-10, using PyTorch. This project is a part of a book being written on the same to document all the research that has been done in this field.

Brain Tumor Radiogenic Classification
